## De architectura, Vitruvio

<div style={{ 
  marginTop: '1.5rem',

**De architectura** (On Architecture) by **Marcus Vitruvius Pollio** is one of the most influential architectural treatises in history. Written in the 1st century BCE, it is the only major work on architecture to survive from classical antiquity.

</div>

<div style={{ 
  marginTop: '1.5rem',
  marginBottom: '1.5rem',
  padding: '1.5rem',
  backgroundColor: 'var(--bg-secondary, #f8f9fa)',
  borderRadius: '8px'
}}>

Vitruvius sought to address the **ethos of architecture**, declaring that quality depends on the **social relevance of the artist's work**, not on the form or workmanship of the work itself.

This profound insight resonates deeply with archUI's philosophy. We believe that a design system's value is not merely in its technical implementation or visual polish, but in its ability to serve meaningful purposes and create genuine value for developers, designers, and end users.

</div>

### The Three Principles

<div style={{ 
  marginTop: '1.5rem',
  marginBottom: '1.5rem',
  padding: '1.5rem',
  backgroundColor: 'var(--bg-secondary, #f8f9fa)',
  borderRadius: '8px'
}}>

Vitruvius established three fundamental principles of architecture that have guided architectural thinking for over two millennia:

- **Firmitas** (Durability): Structures must be solid, stable, and built to last
- **Utilitas** (Utility): Buildings must be functional and serve their intended purpose
- **Venustas** (Beauty): Architecture must be aesthetically pleasing

In archUI, we translate these principles to digital design:

- **Firmitas**: Robust, well-tested components built with TypeScript and modern best practices
- **Utilitas**: Components that solve real problems and enhance developer productivity
- **Venustas**: Beautiful, harmonious designs inspired by classical proportions

</div>

Just as Vitruvius's treatise provided a systematic approach to architecture, ArchUI aims to provide a systematic approach to building digital interfaces. The emphasis on **social relevance** reminds us that our components must serve real needs and create genuine value, not just follow trends or showcase technical prowess.
